# Writing Best Practices — Cross-Venue Master

> [!abstract] What this note is
> A cross-venue synthesis comparing the per-venue masters of **NeurIPS (11 papers), ICML (6), and ICLR (21)** — 38 analysed papers in total. It separates **venue-agnostic best practices** (universal in *every* venue's master — the safest rules to apply to any paper) from **venue-divergent tendencies** (prominent in one venue's corpus but not another's). Generated by `writing-best-practices-comparator` (Mode B); the manual block is preserved.

> [!warning] Read the confound caveat first — it is decisive here
> The three venue folders have **very different genre distributions**: ICML is 67% theory papers, NeurIPS is architecture/empirical-heavy (~45% architecture, only ~18% theory), ICLR is genre-balanced. With gaps this large, **every apparent "venue difference" is confounded by genre mix.** §5 therefore reports a deliberate **null result**: no venue-specific writing tendency can be separated from genre composition at this corpus size. The trustworthy content of this note is §3 (venue-agnostic rules) and §4 (the comparison table). Do not use §5 to choose a submission venue.

## 3. Venue-agnostic best practices

Rules that appear as a **universal rule in all three venue masters** (NeurIPS, ICML, *and* ICLR). These survive across conferences *and* across the genre-mix differences between the folders — which makes them the highest-confidence, most transferable rules in the entire corpus. **Apply these to every paper, any venue.**

> [!success] The venue-agnostic core — apply to every paper
> 1. **Hedge causes, not measurements (Lipton's discrimination).** Universal in all three masters and the single most-cited rule in every venue (NeurIPS, ICML 6/6, ICLR 16/21). State measured/proven results flatly; hedge mechanism, scope, extrapolation. Cited: [[NeurIPS/Writing-Best-Practices#4. Universal rules|NeurIPS #1]], [[ICML/Writing-Best-Practices#4. Universal rules|ICML #2]], [[ICLR/Writing-Best-Practices#4. Universal rules|ICLR #1]].
> 2. **The title encodes the thesis or contribution.** Metaphor, antithesis, question, reframe ("X as Y"), or contribution-sentence with a directional preposition — never a bare topic noun phrase. Universal in all three. Cited: [[NeurIPS/Writing-Best-Practices#4. Universal rules|NeurIPS #4]], [[ICML/Writing-Best-Practices#4. Universal rules|ICML #4]], [[ICLR/Writing-Best-Practices#4. Universal rules|ICLR #4]].
> 3. **Captions and section headers are claims, not legends/topics.** Every figure caption ends with the finding it supports; bold run-in headers state the result. A reader scanning only captions + headers should recover the argument. Universal in all three. Cited: [[NeurIPS/Writing-Best-Practices#4. Universal rules|NeurIPS #2, #9]], [[ICML/Writing-Best-Practices#4. Universal rules|ICML #6]], [[ICLR/Writing-Best-Practices#4. Universal rules|ICLR #5, #7]].
> 4. **Appendix as reviewer insurance.** Every "we used X" in the main paper has an appendix slot — verbatim prompts, robustness sweeps, the experiment that could undermine your own framing, a TOC. Universal in all three. Cited: [[NeurIPS/Writing-Best-Practices#4. Universal rules|NeurIPS #12]], [[ICML/Writing-Best-Practices#4. Universal rules|ICML #7]], [[ICLR/Writing-Best-Practices#4. Universal rules|ICLR #10]].
>
> **Near-universal (universal in 2 of 3 masters explicitly, present in the third as a cross-cutting pattern) — also safe to treat as venue-agnostic:**
> 5. **Name the contribution and reuse the name verbatim.** Explicit universal in ICML (#1) and ICLR (#2); in NeurIPS it appears as a genre-specific move (brand the phenomenon / positional convention) and a macro-architecture pattern. A contribution citers cannot name is one they will not cite.
> 6. **Steelman the worst objection in its own section.** Explicit universal in NeurIPS (#6) and ICLR (#3); ICML expresses it as "frame the result against current practice" + "disclose baseline-favoring choices" (#5, #8).
> 7. **Open the abstract at the specific problem, not a field-level platitude.** Explicit universal in NeurIPS (#7) and ICLR (#6); strongly present though not a standalone universal rule in ICML. Delete "X has achieved remarkable success."

These 7 are the cross-venue playbook. They are the rules with the most evidence behind them in the entire 38-paper corpus.

Venues as columns; each cell summarises that venue corpus's *dominant tendency*.

| Move axis | NeurIPS (11) | ICML (6) | ICLR (21) |
|---|---|---|---|

| Hedging discipline | universal | universal (6/6) | universal (16/21) |
| Title encodes thesis | yes (branded metaphor, finding-list) | yes (antithesis, "X as Y" reframe) | yes (thesis sentence, question, verb) |

## 5. Venue-divergent tendencies

> [!failure] Null result — no venue-specific tendency can be claimed
> Every apparent difference between the three venue corpora is **confounded by genre mix**, and the confound is too large to see through at this corpus size. Concretely:
>
> - **"ICML papers separate a theory leg from an empirical leg and state theorems twice."** — Observed, but ICML's folder is **67% theory papers**, and theory-leg-separation is a *theory-genre* move (see [[ICLR/Writing-Best-Practices#5.1 Theory papers|ICLR's theory playbook]], which says the same thing for ICLR theory papers). ⚠️ **Confounded by genre mix — this is a theory-genre signal, not an ICML signal.**
> - **"NeurIPS papers emphasise two scientific legs, anchor numbers, and negative results."** — Observed, but NeurIPS's folder is architecture/empirical-heavy, and these are architecture/empirical-genre moves. ⚠️ **Confounded — a genre-mix signal, not a NeurIPS signal.**
> - **"ICLR papers freeze notation and organise Related Work by failure mode."** — ICLR's folder is the most genre-balanced, so this is the *least* confounded observation — but ICLR also has the most architecture+theory papers in absolute terms, and notation-freezing is a formal-paper move. ⚠️ **Still confounded; cannot be promoted to an ICLR-specific tendency.**
> - **The "no headline number in the abstract" miss** appears prominently in the ICML and ICLR masters and less so in NeurIPS. Tempting to read as a venue effect — but theory papers (concentrated in ICML) systematically lack a single headline number, so this tracks the **theory-genre share of each folder**, not the venue. ⚠️ **Confounded.**
>
> **Verdict:** *At the current corpus (NeurIPS 11, ICML 6, ICLR 21, with sharply different genre distributions), no venue-specific writing tendency can be separated from genre composition. The venue-agnostic rules in §3 are what you can rely on.*
>
> **To make §5 meaningful in a future regeneration:** grow the folders until their genre distributions overlap — e.g. analyse more theory papers into NeurIPS and more architecture papers into ICML. Once each venue has, say, ≥ 4 papers per major genre, a genre-controlled comparison becomes possible and real venue effects (if any) could surface.

**Coverage gaps that block a trustworthy §5:**
- **ICML theory-heavy, NeurIPS theory-light** — the biggest single confound. Add theory papers to NeurIPS or architecture papers to ICML.
- **tools-system: zero papers in any venue** — analyse a system paper (e.g. vLLM, Mamba) into any folder to open the genre.
- **empirical-scaling thin in ICML (0) and ICLR (1)** — uneven.

Until these gaps close, §5 will remain a null result. §3 does not depend on genre balance and is reliable now.

## 7. How to use this note

- **Writing any paper, any venue:** apply the 7 rules in §3. They are venue-independent and carry the most evidence in the corpus.
- **Writing for a specific venue:** start with §3, then open that venue's per-venue master — [[NeurIPS/Writing-Best-Practices]], [[ICML/Writing-Best-Practices]], or [[ICLR/Writing-Best-Practices]] — for the genre-specific playbook matching your paper.
- **Choosing a submission venue:** §5 does **not** support venue-selection advice at this corpus size — the differences are genre artifacts. Do not use this note to decide where to submit.
- **The one corpus-wide fixable miss:** across all three venues, the most common flagged weakness is **no quotable headline number in the abstract** (Farquhar slot 5) — and, in ICLR, **Figure 1 written as a legend**. Both are free, high-leverage fixes regardless of venue or genre.
